Medical Waste Disposal Management

Ensuring Safe and Environmentally Responsible Waste Disposal in Multi-Speciality Hospitals

Types of Medical Waste

Infectious Waste

Waste contaminated with blood, bodily fluids, or other infectious materials.

Sharps Waste

Needles, syringes, and other sharp objects.

Pharmaceutical Waste

Expired or unused medications and medical supplies.

Chemical Waste

Waste from disinfectants, cleaning agents, and lab chemicals.

Anatomical Waste

Human tissues, organs, and body parts from surgeries.

Radioactive Waste

Materials containing radioactive substances.

Disposal Guidelines

Infectious Waste

Infectious waste should be placed in red biohazard bags and disinfected or sterilized before disposal. Ensure safe handling to avoid contamination.

Sharps Waste

Sharps waste like needles and syringes should be disposed of in puncture-proof containers to prevent injury and contamination.

Pharmaceutical Waste

Unused or expired medications should be disposed of through authorized pharmaceutical waste systems to prevent misuse.

Chemical Waste

Chemical waste should be handled carefully and disposed of at certified hazardous waste facilities to prevent environmental damage.

Anatomical Waste

Anatomical waste requires incineration or special disposal methods, in accordance with health regulations.

Radioactive Waste

Radioactive waste must be disposed of by certified agencies equipped to handle radioactive materials safely.
